# Challenge 2: Value Perception

## The Challenge

> **"List everything your customer GIVES to use your product (not just money), and everything they GET (not just features). Which side is heavier?"**

Value = Benefits minus Costs. Most builders only think about the price tag. They forget that time, effort, learning curve, and anxiety are costs too.

## How to Run This Challenge

### Step 1: Present Findings
Share what the codebase scan revealed about value exchange:
- Onboarding friction: How many steps before first value?
- Signup demands: Email, password, credit card, phone verification?
- Time-to-value: How long until the user achieves their goal?
- Pricing communication: Are benefits clear, or is it just a feature comparison table?
- Hidden costs: Forced account creation, mandatory emails, complex configuration

Example finding:
> "Your signup flow requires email, password, email verification, profile setup, AND a 5-step onboarding wizard before the user sees anything useful. That's 8 minutes of 'paying' before any 'value.' FedEx customers get tracking the moment they hand over the package."

### Step 2: Issue the Challenge
Ask the user to fill this out:

> **What the customer GIVES:**
> - Money: ___
> - Time: ___
> - Effort: ___
> - Personal data: ___
> - Psychological cost (anxiety, risk, trust): ___
>
> **What the customer GETS:**
> - Functional benefit: ___
> - Emotional benefit: ___
> - Social benefit (status, belonging): ___
> - Convenience: ___

### Step 3: Evaluate the Response
Listen for:
- **Red flags:** "Gets" column is all features ("they get real-time sync, dark mode, API access"). Features are not benefits.
- **Yellow flags:** "Gives" column only mentions money. Time and effort are often bigger barriers than price.
- **Green flags:** Clear articulation of emotional/social benefits AND realistic accounting of non-monetary costs.

### Step 4: Push Back
- "You listed 'real-time sync' as a benefit. That's a feature. The BENEFIT is 'never worrying about losing work' or 'always having the latest version without thinking about it.' What's the real benefit?"
- "You forgot the biggest cost: trust. A user has to trust you with their data/workflow. What have you done to earn that trust on first visit?"
- "Your onboarding takes 8 steps. A competitor does it in 2. That's not a feature gap — that's a value gap."
- "FedEx charges more than the postal service, but customers perceive more value because of speed AND peace of mind. What's YOUR 'peace of mind'?"

### Step 5: Record Finding
Document:
- **Risk level:** Red (high friction, unclear value), Yellow (some gaps), Green (clear value, low friction)
- **Evidence:** Specific friction points found in onboarding/signup code, pricing page gaps
- **Recommendation:** Top friction point to eliminate and top benefit to communicate more clearly

## Tone Guide
- YES: "Your user gives you their email, 10 minutes of setup time, and trust — and in return they get... a dashboard? That's not a fair trade."
- NO: "Your onboarding UX needs work."
- YES: "If I'm your customer, I still don't know after 30 seconds on your homepage whether this is worth my time. And time is the most expensive thing I have."
- NO: "You should add more benefits to the landing page."
