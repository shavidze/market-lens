# Challenge 1: Myopia Check

## The Challenge

> **"Describe your product in one sentence — without mentioning a single feature, technology, or implementation detail."**

Most builders can't do this. They say "it's a React app that uses AI to..." instead of "it helps freelancers get paid faster." That gap IS the myopia.

## How to Run This Challenge

### Step 1: Present Findings
Share what you found in the codebase scan — specifically:
- How the README describes the product (feature-first vs problem-first)
- The language used in headings, navigation, and CTAs
- Whether the homepage leads with "what we built" or "what you need"

Example finding:
> "Your README opens with 'A Next.js application with real-time WebSocket support and PostgreSQL backend.' Your customer doesn't care about your stack. They care about what problem you solve."

### Step 2: Issue the Challenge
Ask the user directly:
> "Without using any technical terms, features, or product names — what problem does your product solve, and for whom?"

**Always provide your recommended answer** based on what you found in the codebase scan. The user can accept, reject, or modify it. Example:

> "Based on what I found in your codebase, here's my best guess: '[specific problem] for [specific person].' Does that match your intent, or am I off?"

This saves the user from a blank-page problem and gives them something concrete to react to.

### Step 3: Evaluate the Response
Listen for:
- **Red flags:** Response still contains features ("it lets users create dashboards..."). Features are HOW, not WHY.
- **Yellow flags:** Response is vague ("it helps people be more productive"). Who? More productive at what?
- **Green flags:** Response names a specific person with a specific problem ("freelance designers who waste 3 hours a week chasing invoice payments").

### Step 4: Push Back (Sparring Partner Mode)
If the response has red/yellow flags, challenge them:
- "You said it 'lets users create dashboards' — but that's a feature. What happens in their life if dashboards don't exist?"
- "You said 'helps people be productive' — which people? A CEO and a student have very different productivity problems."
- "The railroad companies thought they were in the train business. They were in the transportation business. What business are YOU really in?"

### Step 5: Record Finding
Document:
- **Risk level:** Red (pure feature-speak), Yellow (mixed), Green (clear problem articulation)
- **Evidence:** Specific quotes from README/UI that show the myopia
- **Recommendation:** Specific rewrite suggestion for the most prominent customer-facing copy

## Tone Guide
Be direct, even provocative — but always constructive:
- YES: "Your homepage reads like a technical spec, not a value proposition. A customer landing here would leave in 5 seconds."
- NO: "Your marketing is bad."
- YES: "You just described 5 features. I still don't know why I should care. Try again."
- NO: "That's wrong, try harder."
